# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Public-sector bodies responsible for winter road maintenance in Austria: regional governments (Landesregierungen), city magistrates (Magistrate), municipalities (Gemeinden), infrastructure operators (Infrastrukturträger), and highway operators such as Asfinag. The people evaluating the site are technical and procurement decision-makers assessing a specialist contractor for a custom, safety-critical installation — they're weighing engineering competence, durability, and dependable long-term support, not comparing consumer options. There is no secondary audience; the site speaks to this one buyer throughout.

## Product Purpose

SG Technik GmbH is an Austrian specialist contractor that plans, builds, and maintains stainless-steel and GFK silo systems and brine (Sole) systems for municipal winter road service. The site exists to generate qualified requests for a free initial consultation from genuine public-sector decision-makers, and to establish SG Technik as the technically credible, dependable partner for a narrow, safety-critical infrastructure category. Success looks like qualified "Erstgespräch" bookings from real buyers, and a site that reads as trustworthy to the high-profile clients already on the roster (Land OÖ, Asfinag, voestalpine, and others).

## Positioning

The Austrian silo- and brine-system specialist that engineers precisely tailored, long-lasting winter-service plants — and stays reachable with fast, hands-on service long after installation.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: "Kostenloses Erstgespräch vereinbaren" (book a free initial consultation). Secondary CTA: "Unsere Leistungen" (view services), for visitors who want to self-educate before committing.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: custom-engineered silo and brine systems, built to last, backed by a 24-hour emergency response.
- Belief ladder: (1) they understand my exact technical requirements, (2) they build genuinely robust, precise, durable systems, (3) they'll respond fast if something breaks (24h on-site support), (4) a free first conversation costs nothing.
- Proof on hand: four named references already in the code (Siloumstellung Steinerkirchen; Neubau 300t Silo Mondsee mit Soleanlage; Neubau 680t Silo Mitterweißenbach mit Soleanlage; Neubau 300t Silo Obernberg mit Soleanlage) plus the customer-type list (Landesregierungen, Magistrate, Gemeinden, Infrastrukturträger, Autobahnbetreiber). The source document (`Aufbau Website 2.0.docx`) also names a client/logo roster not yet reflected on the site: Land OÖ, Stadt Wien, Asfinag, Gemeinde Steinerkirchen, REVA, Bonaventura, Magistrat Linz, voestalpine. ## Brand Personality

Robust, precise, trustworthy. The voice is plainspoken technical B2B German, carried by "Handschlagqualität" — old-school, handshake-quality reliability — rather than sales enthusiasm. Confident but modest: claims are backed by specific numbers (capacities, tolerances, response times) instead of adjectives, and the tone never oversells.

## Anti-references

Not a flashy startup/SaaS site — no gradients, glassmorphism, or hero-metric-dashboard clichés. Not a generic corporate site with bland stock photography and faceless corporate-speak. Not a cheap or DIY tradesman site — it must read as engineering-grade, not amateur or handmade.

## Design Principles

Competence over salesmanship: every section demonstrates technical understanding — specs, tolerances, capacities — rather than leaning on hype or emotional persuasion. Specificity is the proof: real place names, real numbers, and named clients do the convincing that generic claims can't. Low-friction path to the free conversation: since the primary ask costs the visitor nothing, every page should make that step feel easy and un-gatekept, never buried. Earn trust through named proof, not abstraction: lean on actual references, the client roster, and measurable stats ahead of vague quality claims. Respect for practitioners: the audience is technical and procurement people, not consumers, so language stays direct and precise rather than adopting a consumer-marketing register.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target requested; follow general good practice — semantic HTML, sufficient color contrast, full keyboard navigability, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` (already honored in `global.css`).
